# ScopeLock Operations Manager Role

**Version 1.0** | **Department:** Operations | **Reports to:** NLR (Founder)

## Role Overview

The **ScopeLock Operations Manager** is responsible for the day-to-day operations of the ScopeLock team, focusing on team onboarding, technical support, training coordination, and ensuring smooth workflow execution. This role acts as the operational bridge between NLR (strategic/architectural decisions) and the team (daily execution).

**Core Mission:** Enable the team to find jobs, complete missions, and earn money by removing blockers and providing clear guidance.

---

## Key Responsibilities

### 1. Team Onboarding & Setup (30% of time)

**What You Do:**
- Recruit and pitch ScopeLock to potential team members
- Create @mindprotocol.ai email redirects for new members
- Grant Claude team access and send project invitations
- Direct new members to resources (scopelock.mindprotocol.ai/resources)
- Introduce them to AI citizens (Emma, Inna, Rafael, Sofia, Maya, Alexis)
- Verify successful first login and access to all tools

**Deliverables:**
- New member fully onboarded within 24 hours
- Access to: Email, Claude projects, Telegram group, Upwork (if Hunter)
- Initial task assigned and understood

### 2. Technical Support & Troubleshooting (25% of time)

**What You Do:**
- Resolve Upwork connects issues
- Fix access problems (Claude rate limits, login errors)
- Provide workarounds (mobile vs desktop, browser issues)
- Escalate complex technical issues to NLR
- Maintain list of common issues and solutions

**Deliverables:**
- Team member unblocked within 2 hours (during working hours)
- Documentation of recurring issues and fixes
- Escalation to NLR only when truly necessary

### 3. Training & Guidance (20% of time)

**What You Do:**
- Guide job hunting strategies (filters, keywords, volume)
- Review and correct mistakes (wrong job types found)
- Explain ScopeLock concepts when team is confused
- Point to appropriate resources/documentation
- Encourage team to use Kai for questions

**Deliverables:**
- Team members improve over time (fewer repeated mistakes)
- Clear guidance provided within 1 hour of question
- Team members feel supported, not lost

### 4. Progress Monitoring & Coordination (15% of time)

**What You Do:**
- Check in with team members daily
- Monitor job search progress (Hunters)
- Track mission progress (AI Developers)
- Ensure weekly updates are sent to clients (via Reanance/Maya)
- Identify and remove blockers
- Share project links and resources as needed

**Deliverables:**
- Daily progress snapshot (who's working, who's blocked, what's delivered)
- Blockers resolved or escalated within 24 hours
- No team member goes >24 hours without feedback if they ask for help

### 5. Trust & Quality Management (10% of time)

**What You Do:**
- Observe team behavior and flag concerns
- Enforce boundaries (no premature role requests, no tags before trust)
- Give second chances thoughtfully
- Watch for quality issues before they reach clients
- Build psychological safety (mistakes are okay if reported)

**Deliverables:**
- Team trust issues flagged to NLR within 24 hours
- Quality standards maintained
- Team feels safe to report problems
